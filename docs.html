Starbucks Drink Nutrition Explorer – Documentation

Design notes, rationale, and findings for my D3 final project.

← Back to visualization

1. Dataset

I used the public Starbucks drink menu nutrition dataset from Kaggle. It contains nutrition information for different Starbucks drinks and sizes. I focused on the following attributes:

  • Beverage_category – drink category
  • Beverage – drink name
  • Beverage_prep – preparation / size
  • Calories – calories per serving
  • Sugars (g) – sugar in grams
  • Caffeine (mg) – caffeine level

2. Design process

My main question was: “How do calories and sugar relate across different Starbucks drink categories?” I also wanted to understand how caffeine fits into this relationship.

I began with quick pencil sketches exploring different layouts. One early idea involved multiple small charts per category, but I chose a single scatterplot because it clearly shows the relationship between two continuous variables (calories and sugar).

I sketched the layout with controls at the top, the chart as the main focus, and a legend and explanatory notes on the side to create a dashboard-style view.

Initial sketch

3. Visualization & interactions

  • X-axis: Calories
  • Y-axis: Sugar (grams)
  • Color encoding:
    • Drink category (categorical colors)
    • Caffeine level (yellow → red gradient)
  • Interactions:
    • Dropdown to filter by drink category
    • Slider to limit maximum calories
    • Dropdown to switch color encoding
    • Tooltips showing detailed nutrition info

I capped the calorie slider at 600 to prevent extreme outliers from distorting the scale and making comparisons difficult.

4. Design choices & rationale

Encoding

  • Scatterplot: Supports direct comparison of calories versus sugar.
  • Color:
    • Category colors use D3’s schemeTableau10.
    • Caffeine uses a sequential scale to imply intensity.
  • Tooltip: Keeps the chart readable without clutter.

Layout

  • Controls placed at the top act as global filters.
  • Legend and notes help users interpret the visualization.

5. Findings

  • Frappuccino-style drinks cluster in the high-calorie, high-sugar region.
  • Brewed coffee and espresso drinks are low in calories and sugar.
  • Some drinks are high in sugar but relatively low in caffeine.
